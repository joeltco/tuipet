## The through-line

Most of the real damage traces to **two removals whose write-sites were left as
inert citations** — the **mood meter** (`_set_mood` no-op, `current_mood()` can
only return Neutral/Unhappy) and the **obedience meter** (`_set_obedience`
no-op, `obedience` pinned at 0) — plus **the line-bedtime rewrite**, which left a
family of sleep items talking to the old pressure clock. When those pinned
values flow into live formulas, they don't just do nothing — they **silently
bias real outcomes** (a guaranteed −1 on every care grade; a whole tier of
"Happy" rewards made unreachable; three paid sleep items turned to no-ops). The
status-box liveness law has a sibling this audit found missing: *a removed
system must not still bill a live formula.*

## CRIT — earned progress lost / security

### C1. The Digimemory inheritance is a dead end — banked, granted, unredeemable **[corroborated: persistence F1, lifecycle F1]** ✅ v0.5.98
`app.py:383-396` (`_grant_digimemory`) sets `pet.digimemory` and hands the heir
an `"i:32"` chip, but:
- **No code ever reads the payload.** Nothing applies `mem["vaccine"/"data"/
  "virus"/"seconds"]` to any pet (grep: only grant/bank sites exist). The
  applier flow is orphaned — `appactions.py:309` handles an `("inherit", mem)`
  result and `arenafx.py:317` has the fx, but **nothing emits that tuple.**
- **The chip is invisible and inert.** `"i:32"` is not a `shop.CATALOG` key
  (there it's only the *icon* of `revive_floppy`) and not a `vitems.json` key
  (`'i:32' in load_vitems()` → False). The bag renders only keys `shop.entry()`
  resolves, so the chip never shows; `use_item("i:32")` falls to the no-op
  `return ""`. It still inflates the bag header count.
- So the entire death ceremony — etch prompt, "only one" prompt, `bank_digimemory`
  round-trip — feeds a payload the heir can **never** use, while
  `statusbox.py:433` promises "its data can live on in the next egg." It
  re-banks at the heir's own death and circulates forever unusable.

### C2. Quit/crash during the dying beat loses the whole inheritance **[persistence F2]** ✅ v0.5.98
`app.py:860-900`: the etch **and** the `careBonusOnReset` seed are banked *only*
in the `_dying_fx` completion branch. Close the terminal (or crash) during the
~2s dying fx and the relaunch path (`app.py:504-518`) opens the memorial with
`old_mem` only and banks nothing; `action_new` skips `bank_bonus_seed` for a
dead pet. A lifetime of birthday/win-rate bonus is silently dropped and the heir
hatches at `evol_bonus = 0`.

### C3. Poison-mushroom death bypasses the entire death flow **[lifecycle F3]** ✅ v0.5.98
`petcare.py:524-527` (`_deadly`) sets `dead = True` from the bag panel, where the
sim is paused. The death ceremony fires only on a **tick edge** (`app.py:991/1000`:
`was_dead` before `tick`, dying-fx after). By the next tick `was_dead` is already
True — so **no dying fx, no mash-revive window, and the `_dying_fx` completion
block never runs**: `make_digimemory`, `final_care_grade`, `bank_digimemory`,
`bank_bonus_seed` all skipped, bonus lost. Also, unlike `_die()`, it doesn't
clear `asleep`/`hatching`.

### C4. Account switch can destroy the pet's only copy **[persistence F3]** ✅ v0.5.98
`app.py:754-775`: a player with no prior account (or a typo'd new name — the
server *creates* unknown names) reaches `persistence.delete()`, which removes
`save.json` **and** `save.json.bak`, after the park-push was skipped
(`old_name` falsy). Live pet's only copies gone. Same if the park `push_save`
fails (return ignored, line 756). Plus a **stale-cloud clobber**: re-logging into
your *current* account name skips the timestamp guard `sync_down_at_startup`
uses, so a day-old cloud save can overwrite a newer local pet.

### C5. Spoofed `invite_resp` forces a victim into a session — including a **permanent** fusion **[multiplayer F2]** — *verified* ✅ v0.5.98
`lobbyscreen.py:272-276`: an `invite_resp` with `accept:true` calls
`_enter_session(..., host=True)` **with no check that this client ever sent an
invite** to `from_id` (confirmed: no sent-invite tracking exists). Chain: attacker
sends a spoofed accept for `kind:"jogress"` → victim auto-enters and ships its
info → attacker relays a **legacy** payload (no `confirm2`) → the legacy result
screen commits on **any** key incl. ESC (`lobbybout.py:253-258`). Victim's pet is
irreversibly fused (66% energy, DP=0, `evolve_to`) without ever accepting. The
battle variant drags a victim into an unconsented recorded PvP bout. *(The
legacy-commit-on-ESC is by-ruling for mixed-version compat; the defect is the
missing invite-origin check that lets it be weaponized.)*

## HIGH — a paid or central mechanic is broken

### H1. The line-bedtime sleep items are broken for **every current pet** **[corroborated: body-sim F1/F2, economy F1/F2/F3, lifecycle F13]** ✅ v0.5.99
Every hatch binds a line, and line pets sleep by a fixed wall-clock bedtime — not
the `sleep_lapse` pressure meter the old items still poke:
- **Sleep Pill (300b)** `_sleep_pill` force-sleeps, but the next tick sees a line
  pet asleep *outside* its window and `_wake()`s it — **~1 second of sleep**,
  item consumed, can never feed the DP refill.
- **Caffeine Pill (300b)** nudges `sleep_lapse`, which line pets never read —
  **a paid no-op.**
- **Music Player (300b)** grants no `_bed_postpone_t`, so inside the window the
  pet re-sleeps next tick — the purpose-built alarm is **weaker than throwing any
  other item** (which routes through `_disturbed`'s real grace).

### H2. `final_care_grade` is broken at both ends of the stage curve **[corroborated: body-sim F4, lifecycle F4]** ✅ v0.5.99
`pet.py:875-884` `_growth_period` sums `STAGE_DURATION["Mega"] = 9e9` (a
"never auto-evolves" sentinel). So `b += int((age - growth)/DAY_MINUTES)`:
- **Every Mega grades 0** (`≈ int(-9e9/1440)` → `max(0,b)=0`) — the best life in
  the game always seeds the heir +0.
- **Every non-Mega elder over-grades** by +hundreds (Ultimate: growth ~7080s vs
  lifespan ~260k–430k s → **+175..+295**), swamping the ±1 fine structure the
  rest of the function is built on and feeding `evolve_to`'s lifespan bonus and
  the Digimemory etch.

### H3. The whole surrender / perfect-wins / escape cluster is caller-less, with a false comment **[combat F3]** ✅ v0.5.99
`petbattle.py:303-309` `surrender_reject` sets `_surr_declined = True` "# consumed
by record_battle" — but `record_battle` never reads it, and
`SURR_DECLINED_LOST_OBED` is never applied. Repo-wide, `surrender_reject`,
`surrender_effect`, `check_surrender`, `can_escape`, `_check_perfect_wins` have
**zero callers**; `Battle.surrender` (battle.py:234) lacks the `if self.over`
guard `_finish` has. The comment is flatly false.

### H4. Effort heal evaporates next tick and bills a missed-day **[body-sim F5]** ✅ v0.5.99
`petbody.py:370-377`: `_str_t` keeps accruing while `strength == 0` (the decay
branch requires `strength > 0`, nothing resets it). Feed a Vitamin/pill after the
gauge has sat empty past `~3000s` → next tick `_str_t >= interval` fires,
`strength` drops straight back to 0 **and `mistake_day += 1`.** The heal is undone
in one second and the day is marked. Fix: reset `_str_t` on refill or while at 0.

### H5. Offline catch-up runs a different, harsher sim than the live tick **[corroborated: body-sim F6, persistence F4/F5/F6/F7]** ✅ v0.5.99
`persistence._offline` (persistence.py:739-781) ignores the gates the live tick
honors and runs the wrong rates:
- **~6× harsher.** Offline hunger = 1 heart/300s vs live ~1800s (**6.0×**); poop =
  1/480s vs live ~2700s (**5.6×**). Quitting is strictly worse than idling —
  inverting the module's own "gentle" contract.
- **Ignores `asleep`** (offline can starve a sleeping pet *and* bill a care
  mistake — a state live sleep can't produce), **`full_until`** (Steak's 12h
  satiety void while away — exactly when bought), **`auto_clean_until`** (Port.
  Potty's 24h auto-clean void while away).
- **Age runs, evolution doesn't.** `age_seconds += elapsed` up to 36h but
  `stage_seconds` frozen → a near-elder left closed 36h can die of "old age" one
  tick after the welcome, having gained zero stage progress. The "never death
  offline" header promise is only *deferred*, not kept.
- **Sleep earns nothing offline** — no energy, no DP, no `awake_lapse`; a full
  night away restores nothing.

### H6. Tournament ESC at the timing bar = a silent forfeit-loss, hinted as "back out" **[combat F2]** ✅ v0.5.99
`battlescreen.py:329-334`: ESC in `ready` returns `("done", None)`;
`tournamentscreen.py:50-57` reads `r[1]` as None → `record(False)` → real
elimination (consolation math, cup over, stake gone). The strip for that exact
state says `("ESC","back out")`, and the *raid* host treats the identical signal
as "You back off. The attempt keeps." Same signal, three meanings — in the cup,
"back out" before the bell is a stake-losing forfeit.

## MED — a system misfires in a reachable case

### M1. `current_mood()` can never be "Happy" — a whole reward tier is unreachable **[corroborated: body-sim F3, lifecycle F6]** ✅ v0.5.100
`pet.py:789-794` returns only Unhappy/Neutral. Dead consumers: the **good
birthday** (+lifespan, +evol_bonus, the Cupcake — a birthday-only item),
`record_battle`'s `BONUS_ATTRIBUTE_POWER` doubling, `_special_idle`'s happy
bounce, `final_care_grade`'s `m=="Happy"` +1, `_wake`'s terrible-morning pose.
Perfect care still tops out at the plain Cookie day.

### M2. `final_care_grade` unconditionally docks −1 via the dead obedience meter **[corroborated: body-sim F13, lifecycle F5]** ✅ v0.5.100
`pet.py:902-905`: `obedience` pinned at 0 (`_set_obedience` no-op, no writers),
`BONUS_DEC_OBEDIENCE=50` → the `elif obedience < 50: b -= 1` fires on **every**
life graded. A removed system permanently docking the heir's seed. (Also makes
`_calc_to_nap`'s `obedience >= 75` discount unreachable — every pet pays the +1
doze delay.)

### M3. Line jogress doors can't open with their own chart partners **[lifecycle F2]** — *executed repro* ✅ v0.5.100
`jogress.py:183-193` `_distinct_component` demands the partner be a *corpus-graph*
base of the target, but the doors' contract is "any same-stage partner with a
listed attribute." Real-data repro: Tortamon 113 + Gatomon 100 (both charts
declare the Jagamon 226 door mutually) → the Tortamon side's `resolve_online`
returns **None** → the lobby aborts both sides → **no fusion ever fires between
declared partners.** 41 of 147 attribute doors fail with every co-declaring chart
partner. Exact-dex doors (Omnimon Alter-S) verified working.

### M4. Battle replay suppresses a real applied hit; engine contradicts its own "no retaliation" rule **[combat F1]** — *verified* ✅ v0.5.100
`battle.py:201-206` applies both sides' hits; `battlescreen.py:90-97` drops the
foe's strike from the *animation* whenever the pet's blow zeroes the foe — but
`edmg` was already subtracted from `pet_hp`. The pet loses 1-2 HP on-record with
no animation; the next between-round page (lobbybout PvP replay) shows the true
lower HP after a replay that showed no hit.

### M5. Tournament & remote-invite bouts skip the condition gates the send-side enforces **[corroborated: combat F4/F5, multiplayer F12]** ✅ v0.5.100
`can_battle` refuses on hunger 0 / low energy / sick / poop; the lobby runs it
before *sending* a PvP invite. But `tournament.can_enter` and `_session_gate`
(the accept side) check only dead/asleep/Egg-Fresh. A starving, sick pet too
drained to *send* a challenge can grind three recorded cup battles per cup **and**
auto-accept incoming invites. Plus `_session_gate("jogress")` can trigger a
visible `refuse` animation from a stranger's invite — a hairline crack in "a
stranger's invite must never touch the pet."

### M6. Raid screen leaks a live lobby connection → mutual-eviction reconnect loop **[multiplayer F1]** — *verified* ✅ v0.5.100
`appactions.py`: `_after_raid` never cancels the raid's `LobbyClient` worker (it's
a **live** login), and a later lobby login overwrites the handle, orphaning it.
Both sessions share the process-wide `BOOT`, so the server's newer-session guard
(`c.boot > boot`) never fires — each login *evicts* the other with `error` (not
`login_failed`, so `_stop` isn't set) → reconnect backoff → re-evict, for the rest
of the run. Open RAID, ESC, open LOBBY → "reconnecting…" churn.

### M7. Crafted oversized `pet` card is a standing lobby DoS **[multiplayer F4]** ✅ v0.5.100 — droplet deployed 2026-07-19 (server _clamp_pet live, pm2 restart clean)
`server.py:996` stores `m.get("pet")` verbatim, no size/field clamp, and
rebroadcasts it in every roster. Inbound cap is 64KB; client read cap is 256KB.
~5 crafted logins push every roster past 256KB → every honest client dies with
1009, reconnects, dies again until the attacker leaves.

### M8. Ladder losses are voidable at will **[multiplayer F3]** ✅ v0.5.100
`lobbybout.py`: the report files only in `_battle_over` (the final-round
keypress); ESC in "fight" is `_forfeit` → relay `abort`, **no report**. A losing
player (or a crafted client that knows the seeded outcome) ESCs before the last
key; the winner's report expires unconfirmed. The ladder only credits wins
against players who politely finish.

### M9. DigiCore meter freezes at 14 for any Mega with onward rows **[lifecycle F7]** ✅ v0.5.100
`digicore.py:40-50`: for Mega, `growth = 9e9` makes `pending` True and
`n = 14` forever — the lifespan count-up meter the docstring promises a final
form never appears.

### M10. Data-book evolution list sorted backwards + a permanently-unmet item row **[corroborated: lifecycle F9/F10]** ✅ v0.5.100
`digicore.py:185` sorts corpus candidates **ascending** by fulfilled score, so the
book's "closest-first" top row is the one the engine is *least* likely to pick —
disagreeing with the silhouette on the same screen. And `evolution.py:527-531`
checks an `i:N` key against a bag that stores *named* keys, so a held Digimental's
row always reads unmet, inflating every item-locked form's unmet count.

### M11. A cancelled retire leaves duplicate headstones + a premature snapshot **[lifecycle F8]** ✅ v0.5.100
`appactions.py:358-368`: `action_new` calls `snapshot_prev_gen` (appends a legacy
headstone, overwrites `last_gen`) *before* the egg carousel — but ESC-ing the
carousel cancels the hatch without undoing it. Repeat N→ESC and each attempt
appends another headstone for the same life; a still-live pet gets recorded as the
"previous generation" for egg gates.

### M12. `select()`'s never-stuck fallback can force an antibody-less pet into an Induced-X form **[lifecycle F12]** ✅ v0.5.100
`evolution.py:352-356`: the fallback filters `special` and item locks but **not**
`xantibody == "Induced"`, which `check()`/`divergence_*` all enforce. A corpus
pet with no passing candidate can be pushed into an Induced form, then
`evolve_to` locks in `x_antibody = "Permanent"` — an X form + free antibody with
no X egg or chip. 92 corpus forms qualify.

## SUSPECT — RULED 2026-07-20 ("do S"), shipped v0.5.102

- **S1. `save_from_death` jumps a *young* pet's age to `lifespan - REVIVAL_LIFE`
  (~750s before death)** — a 2500b Rev.Floppy or a mash-rescue on a sickness/
  starvation/poison/neglect death silently burns nearly the whole remaining
  lifespan, unstated anywhere. Canon-shaped for old-age deaths only. **[body-sim]**
  ✅ v0.5.102 — RULED a defect: "RevivalLifeInc *restores* life" is the canon
  word, so the revival now FLOORS remaining life at REVIVAL_LIFE
  (`age = min(age, lifespan − REVIVAL_LIFE)`) — old-age deaths unchanged, a
  young rescue keeps its life.
- **S2. DNA wager charged at mash-start, awarded at mash-end** — an autosave/crash
  mid-mash persists the charge with no award. The tournament documents the same
  shape as a deliberate forfeit; the wager has no such ruling. **[economy F10]**
  ✅ v0.5.102 — RULED: charge-at-start *stands* (canon DVPet onEnter, and a
  refund would be free re-roll insurance on a mash going badly, the
  tournament-forfeit logic) — but the charge must buy an outcome: the paid
  mash is marked in flight (`dna_wager_pending`, persisted) and a relaunch
  SETTLES it as a spoiled mash (rate 0; a ≥500 stake still stabilizes), with
  a load message saying so.
- **S3. Etch-then-Keep is lose-lose** — E then K discards `new_mem` but keeps the
  *lower* post-etch seed; a player wanting the elder kept is strictly better
  pressing B first, and nothing says so. **[lifecycle F17]**
  ✅ v0.5.102 — RULED a defect: K at the only-one prompt now re-banks the KEPT
  grade exactly like B at the first prompt — the discarded etch's bonus
  carries; E→K and B are equivalent, as a real choice must be.
- **S4. Process-suspend (Ctrl-Z / laptop sleep) drops time entirely** — no ticks,
