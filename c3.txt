Polish the lobby chat: cell-width safety, the announcement line, and mute

Rendered the chat across its real states and looked at it. Three things were
wrong.

THE CELL-WIDTH LAW. The lobby is the one screen where a player types arbitrary
text, and an emoji or CJK glyph is TWO terminal cells wide while counting as ONE
character. _fit was `s[:w].ljust(w)` and _wrap measured with len(), so a line
like "🔥🔥 emoji test 🐉 wide chars 日本語テキスト" overflowed its 25-column budget
and shoved the roster divider │ right off its column -- the layout visibly tore,
in a text chat, where people type emoji constantly. Every width decision now
goes through rich's cell_len / set_cell_size / chop_cells, so wide glyphs wrap
whole and never split down the middle. The input line scrolled with a
character-based tail slice too, so typed emoji could run past the frame; it
scrolls by cells now.

THE ANNOUNCEMENT WAS HIDING IN THE CHATTER. A dev announcement -- a new release,
a heads-up -- rendered in plain INK as "📢: text": visually identical to ordinary
chatter, and reading like a PLAYER NAMED 📢 was talking. It is the loudest thing
in the room. It now renders bright and without the fake name-colon.

BLOCKING WAS HALF TRUE. net.py filters a blocked peer's chat and PMs going
forward, but everything they had ALREADY said stayed on screen -- so "Blocked X."
left the spam you just muted sitting right there. A mute means MUTE: their lines
are swept from the log, their unread badge cleared. Other peers, join/leave
notices and the dev's line are untouched.

Also: the empty-room hint was clipped mid-sentence ("— say hi, the room hears"),
because it was truncated with [:cw] against a column too narrow for it. It now
picks a line that fits and centres it.

1,242 tests green; test_lobby_chat_polish.py pins the divider column against
emoji/CJK, the input-line overflow, the 📢 styling, and that a mute never eats
anyone else's lines.
